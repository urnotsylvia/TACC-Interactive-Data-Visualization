Project Group 08: TACC, DS 4200 S22

Jonathan Moore, Xi Cao, Natalie Hammel

Service-Learning Course Project as part of Ds 4200 S22 Information Presentation & Visualization, taught by Prof. Cody Dunne, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

Housing issues based on racial disparities and income inequality continue to rise in Boston. In particular, The American City Coalition (TACC) has identified Dorchester, Roxbury, Downtown Boston, and South Boston Waterfront as areas of interest. TACC is a non-profit innovative consultancy organization based in the Boston neighborhood of Roxbury, grounded in an inclusive, comprehensive, and collaborative approach to neighborhood revitalization.

Our mission was to display Boston housing data relating to racial demographics, income distribution, and housing type. These graphs are intended to provide TACC and their partners with an overview of Dorchester, Roxbury, South Boston Waterfront, and Downtown Boston. We hope these graphs are found to be a concise display of the given, aggregated data. These visualizations are expected to be used for internal distribution and communication within TACC and between TACC and relevant partners.

Visualization


Visualization Explanation

We propose the visualization above to display this data. The pie charts display the racial breakup in our 4 neighborhoods of interest. We decided to display the piecharts in a bubble chart format, such that the area of each pie chart is determined by the population size. By displaying population size by area instead of by radius, we are able to address a potential Lie Factor issue. Additionally, we have a legend on the right-hand slide and a tooltip enabled such that percentages are easily found through mouse hover. We hope this pie chart is able to address TACC's need to display racial group data for their neighborhoods of interest.

To continue investigating the housing disparities between the neighborhoods, we also created a histogram to display the income distribution of each neighborhood. The user is able to view each of the histograms by clicking on the corresponding pie chart. For furthur insight, the user is able to hover above each bar to find the numeric data. This histogram, enabled with linking, is able to present income distribution information in tandem with racial and population data.

Finally, we created a stacked bar chart in the lower right hand corner to display information relating to homeownership type. This information is relevant to see in relationt to racial group and income distribution data. The stacked bar chart is able to reveal more information through a tooltip mousehover.

All 3 of these visualizations work together to display various characteristics of 4 different neighborhoods in Boston. The user is able to see general trends in income distribution, racial breakup, and housing ownership. We believe this visualization thus addresses our Analyze/High-Level Task of 'Present'.
